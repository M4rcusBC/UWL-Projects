RUSH FOR WORKS ON ECLIPSES

Library Unable To Supply Demand For the Past Three Days.

While the eclipse has possibly been uppermost in the minds of all Londoners for the past week or so and the word eclipse is a by-word in almost every household and business establishment, the prominence of the solar phenomenon has been felt the keenest at the public library. Librarian R. E. Crouch states that hundreds of people have invaded the library during the past three or four days seeking infomration on the eclipse and the library has been unable to supply that demand. Those who left their visit to the library to the last couple of days were disappointed because all of the books in the institution were out some 10 days earlier.

We have about 75 volumes pertaining to the exlipses in the library, and all of those were out nearly two weeks ago, states Librarian Crouch. We have literally been swamped with callers seeking information on this matter and we were not able to supply near the demand for literature which the phenomenon has caused.

BUD GREY BACK FROM COAST

Salt Lake Trick Bike Rider Now Heading Three Team Combination.

Bud Grey, the Salt Lake boy who has made a name for humself as a trick rider, arrived in the city yesteday after a year's abscence on the Pacific coast. Grey is now at the head of a three-team combination doing fancy stunts and tricks on a bicycle. With him are Fravilo, the girl who looped the loop in a ball at the Salt Palace a couple of seasons ago, and Zarrow, a comedian trick rider.

Grey is well known locally and is one of the few trick riders who has performed at the saucer track. He first began trick t riding in this city about five years ago and is now at the top of his profession. Grey was around visiting friends yesterday and is now making arrangements to put his turn on in Salt Lake. From here he goes east, where he is booked ahead for the summer season.
